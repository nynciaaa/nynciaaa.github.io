Rich Brown Website Review
1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I noticed when interacting with the experience was the loading screen upon opening the page. Personally, I found the flickering and flashing letters of the Rich Brown name overwhelming and slightly straining on my eyes. The loading screen could have been more effective without the randomized lettering of his name. However, aside from that, the transition into his website was visually appealing.

    • 3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

    I spent most of my time scrolling through Rich Brown's website and exploring his content. He visually conveyed a lot of information in a structured and engaging way, ensuring it wasn’t overwhelming to read. The site’s layering and sectioning were well designed. Additionally, the animations, such as his work sliding in from the sides and text moving or tilting as I scroll further down, thereby enhancing the experience. Overall, apart from the loading screen, the aesthetic was perfect.

    4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

    The most common action I performed was scrolling through the website. It caught my attention because it was highly interactive and engaging. The transitions, animations, and visuals matched his aesthetic well. As I scrolled further, I could see previews of his work by hovering over different elements.

    5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

    I believe Rich Brown's primary goal was to showcase his portfolio, including his projects, works, and awards, to attract potential clients or collaborators. He did an excellent job making his website visually striking with a modern aesthetic. The combination of clean visuals, subtle animations, and smooth transitions made the interface feel responsive and engaging without overwhelming the user. By allowing users to hover over his works and see previews, he effectively encourages exploration and engagement.

    6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

    Rich Brown's interactive experience communicates his goal by allowing users to engage with his content actively. Hovering over his titles and visual elements reveals previews, while clicking on them takes users to a different page for a full view of his work. This interactive structure ensures that visitors remain engaged while exploring his portfolio.

    7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

    I believe Rich Brown's website is designed for multiple visits, especially for users interested in his portfolio. The engaging animations, structured layout, and interactive elements encourage visitors to return, whether to view his latest projects, revisit specific works, or learn more about his awards and achievements. The website is not a one time experience and it can be interacted during extended browsing sessions.

    8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

    The website encourages multiple visits through its interactive elements. The hover effects, animated transitions, and project previews suggest that users can return and still discover new details they might have missed earlier. The structured layout, where information is layered rather than presented all at once, indicates that users can engage at their own pace rather than consuming everything in a single visit.

The smooth transitions and interactive elements are reminiscent of contemporary web design used in creative portfolios and agencies.

    12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

    The most frustrating part of the interaction was the white line that trails the cursor. I found it distracting, as it didn't move smoothly and was unsatisfying to look at. Additionally, some users might become too focused on the cursor effect rather than the website's content. I had a friend interact with the website, and they became so distracted by the cursor trail that they werent paying attention to the actual content. They had to restart from the top to fully engage with the website.

    13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

    The most satisfying part of the experience was scrolling through the page and observing the animations as information transitioned in and out. I appreciated the overall aesthetic of the website, as I prefer minimalist and modern designs. The colour choices, theme, and animations were well balanced, making the website easy to read and visually appealing. The layout was structured well, ensuring that information wasnt bulky or overwhelming.
